## SECTION A: Major Crop Diseases

### A1. Rice Blast — ✅ EXAMPLE (fully filled)

**Scientific Name:** Pyricularia oryzae (Magnaporthe oryzae)
**Crop Affected:** Rice (all varieties, especially high-yield)
**Type:** Fungal
**Season/Conditions:** Kharif season, high humidity (>90%), temperature 25-28°C, overcast weather, excessive nitrogen application

**Symptoms:**
- **Leaf blast:** Diamond/spindle-shaped spots with grey center and brown margin on leaves
- **Neck blast:** Infection at panicle base — neck turns brown/black, grain filling stops
- **Node blast:** Blackish lesions on nodes, stem breaks
- **Early stage:** Small blue-green specks that enlarge within days

**Economic Impact:** Can cause 50-90% yield loss in severe outbreaks. Neck blast is most destructive.

**Organic/Traditional Treatment:**
- Spray Pseudomonas fluorescens (2.5 kg/ha) as preventive
- Tricyclazole-free: Apply neem oil + Trichoderma viride
- Maintain proper spacing — avoid dense planting
- Drain fields periodically to reduce humidity

**Chemical Treatment:**
- **Preventive:** Tricyclazole 75 WP @ 0.6 g/L (spray at tillering + booting stage)
- **Curative:** Isoprothiolane 40 EC @ 1.5 mL/L
- **Alternative:** Carbendazim 50 WP @ 1 g/L

**Prevention:**
- Use resistant varieties (Pusa 44, IR-64)
- Balanced fertilization — don't over-apply nitrogen
- Remove and burn infected stubble after harvest
- Treat seeds with Carbendazim before sowing
