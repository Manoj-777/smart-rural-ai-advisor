# Government Schemes for Indian Farmers — Comprehensive Guide

> This knowledge base is used by the Smart Rural AI Advisor to answer farmer queries about government schemes,
> eligibility, benefits, documents required, and how to apply. All data is sourced from official portals:
> farmer.gov.in, pmkisan.gov.in, pmfby.gov.in, agricoop.nic.in, and respective scheme websites.
> Figures are for 2025-26 unless otherwise noted.

## 1. PM-KISAN — Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi

### Overview
- **Launched:** February 2019 by Government of India
- **Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare
- **Objective:** Direct income support to all landholding farmer families
- **Amount:** ₹6,000 per year in 3 equal installments of ₹2,000 each
- **Payment Schedule:** April–July, August–November, December–March
- **Beneficiaries:** ~11 crore farmer families
- **Total Disbursed (till 2025):** Over ₹3.04 lakh crore across 18+ installments

### Eligibility
- **Who qualifies:**
  - All landholding farmer families (with cultivable land in their name)
  - All small and marginal farmers (up to 2 hectares)
  - Large farmers also eligible (no land size limit for general category)
- **Who does NOT qualify:**
  - Institutional landholders (trusts, companies)
  - Current/former MPs, MLAs, Mayors, Ministers
  - Central/State government employees (except Class IV/Multi-Tasking Staff)
  - Income Tax payers (previous assessment year)
  - Professionals like doctors, engineers, lawyers, CAs with practice income
  - Retired pensioners with monthly pension > ₹10,000

### Documents Required
1. Aadhaar card (mandatory — linked to bank account)
2. Bank account details (Aadhaar-linked)
3. Land ownership records (Khasra/Khatauni, Patta, RoR)
4. Mobile number
5. State-specific land certificates (if applicable)

### How to Apply
1. **Online:** Visit pmkisan.gov.in → "New Farmer Registration" → Enter Aadhaar + State → Fill form → Upload documents
2. **Offline:** Visit nearest Common Service Centre (CSC) or local agriculture office
3. **Through Village Level Entrepreneur (VLE):** CSC operators help with registration
4. **Check status:** pmkisan.gov.in → "Beneficiary Status" → Enter Aadhaar or account number

### Tips for Farmers
- Keep Aadhaar linked to bank account (mandatory for DBT)
- If installment is delayed, check eKYC status on portal — face authentication or OTP-based eKYC mandatory
- Land records must be updated with Revenue Department
- Report issues at PM-KISAN helpline: **155261** or **011-24300606**
- Mobile app: **PM-KISAN Mobile App** (available on Google Play)

## 2. PMFBY — Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (Crop Insurance)

### Overview
- **Launched:** Kharif 2016 (replaced National Agricultural Insurance Scheme)
- **Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare
- **Objective:** Provide comprehensive crop insurance coverage against crop loss/damage due to unforeseen events at affordable premiums
- **Premium (Farmer's Share):**
  - Kharif crops: **2%** of sum insured
  - Rabi crops: **1.5%** of sum insured
  - Commercial & Horticulture crops: **5%** of sum insured
- **Government pays:** Remaining premium (shared equally between Centre & State)
- **Coverage:** 27+ crops across all states (some states opted out and returned later)
- **Beneficiaries:** ~4.5 crore farmer applications per year

### Eligibility
- **Who qualifies:**
  - All farmers — landowners, tenant farmers, sharecroppers growing notified crops in notified areas
  - Both loanee and non-loanee farmers
- **Mandatory for:** Not mandatory since Kharif 2020 — made voluntary for all farmers including loanee farmers (earlier was mandatory for crop loan borrowers)
- **Voluntary for:** All categories of farmers including those with KCC/crop loans

### Coverage
- **Pre-sowing:** Prevented sowing/planting risk — insured area cannot be sown due to deficit rainfall or adverse seasonal conditions (coverage up to 25% of sum insured)
- **Standing crop (Sowing to Harvest):** Non-preventable risks — drought, dry spell, flood, inundation, widespread pest/disease attack, natural fire, storm, hailstorm, cyclone, typhoon, tempest, hurricane, tornado
- **Post-harvest:** Coverage for up to 14 days after harvesting for crops left in "cut and spread" condition in the field — against cyclone, cyclonic rains, unseasonal rains
- **Localized calamities:** Hailstorm, landslide, inundation affecting isolated farms (individual assessment basis, not area-based)
- **Mid-season adversity:** On-account payment up to 25% of likely claim for mid-season adversity
- **NOT covered:** War, nuclear risks, malicious damage, theft, acts of enmity, damage by domestic/wild animals, post-harvest losses during storage/transport

### Claim Process
1. **Report loss within 72 hours** — via Crop Insurance App, toll-free number 14447, insurance company branch, bank, or local agriculture officer
2. **Assessment:** Insurance company appoints surveyor within 48 hours of claim intimation; uses technology (remote sensing, drone, CCE data) for yield estimation
3. **Documents:** Fill claim form, attach land records, sowing certificate, bank account details, photos of damage
4. **Settlement:** Claims settled within 2 months of yield data finalization; amount = (Threshold Yield − Actual Yield) / Threshold Yield × Sum Insured; payment via DBT to Aadhaar-linked bank account

### Documents Required
1. Aadhaar card
2. Bank account passbook (Aadhaar-linked)
3. Land records (Khasra/Khatauni) or lease agreement for tenant farmers
4. Sowing certificate from Patwari/Revenue officer
5. Crop damage photographs (for localized calamity claims)
6. FIR or panchnama (for specific calamity claims)
7. Premium payment receipt

### How to Apply
1. **Online:** Visit pmfby.gov.in → Register → Select State, District, Block, Crop → Pay premium → Get policy
2. **Through bank:** If you have a crop loan (KCC), the bank can enroll you (opt-in/opt-out option available)
3. **CSC centres:** Common Service Centres can register farmers
4. **Mobile app:** "Crop Insurance" app on Google Play — register, calculate premium, report losses
5. **Deadline:** Must enroll before the cut-off date announced for each crop/season (typically 2 weeks before sowing window closes)

### Key Numbers
- **Helpline:** **14447** (toll-free, all languages)
- **App:** **Crop Insurance App** (Google Play) — premium calculator, claim filing, crop loss reporting
- **Website:** pmfby.gov.in
- **WhatsApp:** Some states have WhatsApp-based claim reporting

## 3. KCC — Kisan Credit Card

### Overview
- **Launched:** 1998, revamped multiple times (latest: KCC Saturation Drive 2020+)
- **Ministry:** Ministry of Finance + Ministry of Agriculture
- **What:** Short-term crop loan + working capital for agriculture at subsidized interest; operates like a revolving credit facility
- **Interest Rate:** 7% per annum; with prompt repayment subvention → effective rate of **4% per annum** (3% interest subvention by GoI for loans up to ₹3 lakh)
- **Loan Limit:** Based on Scale of Finance × Area cultivated + 10% post-harvest/household consumption + 20% maintenance of farm assets. Maximum limit without collateral: **₹1.60 lakh**
- **Validity:** 5 years with annual renewal; limit increases by 10% each year for crop loan component

### Eligibility
- **Who qualifies:**
  - All farmers — owner cultivators, tenant farmers, oral lessees, sharecroppers
  - Self Help Groups (SHGs) and Joint Liability Groups (JLGs) of farmers
  - Fishermen (for fish/shrimp cultivation) — included since 2019
  - Animal husbandry farmers (dairy, poultry, etc.) — included since 2019
- **Age:** 18–75 years (co-borrower required above 60 years)
- **No income limit** — available for all farmer categories

### Benefits
1. **Low interest:** Effective 4% p.a. (with subvention) — cheapest formal credit for farmers
2. **Flexible withdrawal:** ATM-enabled RuPay debit card — withdraw anytime from any ATM
3. **No collateral:** Up to ₹1.60 lakh loan without any collateral or mortgage
4. **Crop insurance:** PMFBY premium can be debited from KCC account
5. **Allied activities:** Covers dairy, fisheries, sericulture, beekeeping — not just crops
6. **One-time documentation:** No need to apply every season; revolving credit for 5 years
7. **Crop loan + term loan:** Single card for short-term crop needs and medium-term investment

### How to Apply
1. **Visit any bank branch:** Nearest branch of any commercial bank, cooperative bank, or Regional Rural Bank (RRB)
2. **Documents needed:**
   - Aadhaar card + PAN card (PAN for loans above ₹1.60 lakh)
   - Land records (Khasra/Khatauni, Patta, or tenant/lease agreement)
   - Passport-size photos
   - Application form (available at bank)
   - Crop sowing details/plan
3. **Processing:** Bank verifies land records, approves within 14 days
4. **Online:** Some banks offer KCC application via their websites (SBI, PNB, etc.)
5. **PM-KISAN beneficiaries:** Special drive to issue KCC to all PM-KISAN registered farmers

### Repayment
- **Crop loan:** Repay within 12 months (aligned with crop cycle)
- **Interest subvention benefit:** Repay within 1 year to get 4% effective rate; if late, full 7% applies
- **Additional 2% subvention:** Prompt repayment incentive — total subvention can reach 5%, making effective rate **2% in some cases** (announced periodically)
- **Penalty:** If overdue beyond 1 year, classified as NPA; no interest subvention; higher penalty rate applies
- **Restructuring:** In case of crop failure/natural calamity, banks may convert crop loan to term loan with extended repayment

## 4. Soil Health Card Scheme

### Overview
- **Launched:** February 2015
- **Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare
- **Objective:** Issue Soil Health Cards to all farmers every 2 years — providing soil nutrient status and fertilizer recommendations to improve productivity and reduce input costs
- **Cards issued:** Over 23 crore Soil Health Cards distributed across India (Cycle I + Cycle II + III)
- **Cost to farmer:** **FREE** — fully funded by government
- **Grid-based sampling:** Soil collected in a grid pattern — 2.5 hectare grid for irrigated areas, 10 hectare grid for rainfed areas

### What the Card Shows
- **12 parameters tested:**
  1. **pH** — Soil acidity/alkalinity
  2. **Electrical Conductivity (EC)** — Salinity level
  3. **Organic Carbon (OC)** — Soil fertility indicator
  4. **Nitrogen (N)** — Available nitrogen
  5. **Phosphorus (P)** — Available phosphorus
  6. **Potassium (K)** — Available potassium
  7. **Sulphur (S)** — Secondary nutrient
  8. **Zinc (Zn)** — Micronutrient
  9. **Iron (Fe)** — Micronutrient
  10. **Copper (Cu)** — Micronutrient
  11. **Manganese (Mn)** — Micronutrient
  12. **Boron (B)** — Micronutrient
- **Colour coding:** Green (sufficient), Yellow (moderately deficient), Red (deficient) for each parameter
- **Fertilizer recommendation:** Specific NPK and micronutrient dosage for the crops farmer plans to grow

### How to Get One
1. **Through Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK):** Visit nearest KVK — soil samples collected and tested in their labs
2. **Agriculture Department:** State agriculture officer or block-level agriculture office
3. **Village-level soil collection:** Trained soil sampling agents (Krishi Sakhis, Soil Health Volunteers) collect from fields
4. **Online:** Visit soilhealth.dac.gov.in → Enter state/district → Download your card (if already tested)
5. **Mobile:** Soil Health Card App — scan QR code on card for digital access

### How to Use the Recommendations
- **Follow the card's fertilizer prescription** — it tells exactly how much Urea, DAP, MOP, and micronutrient to apply per acre/hectare for specific crops
- **Savings:** Farmers following Soil Health Card recommendations save ₹2,000–₹5,000 per hectare on fertilizers
- **Organic carbon management:** If OC is low, card recommends adding FYM, compost, or green manure
- **Integration with KCC:** Banks increasingly ask for Soil Health Card data when approving crop loans (helps justify input costs)
- **Crop selection:** If soil is saline (high EC), card suggests salt-tolerant crops; if acidic, suggests liming

### Key Numbers
- **Portal:** soilhealth.dac.gov.in
- **Helpline:** Contact state agriculture department or nearest KVK
- **Frequency:** New card every 2 years (government target)

## 5. eNAM — National Agriculture Market

### Overview
- **Launched:** April 2016
- **Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare
- **Objective:** Create a pan-India electronic trading portal linking all APMC mandis for transparent price discovery, online bidding, and seamless inter-state trade of agricultural commodities
- **Operator:** Small Farmers' Agribusiness Consortium (SFAC)
- **Commodities:** 200+ commodities including cereals, pulses, oilseeds, fruits, vegetables, spices, fibre, and others
- **Language support:** Available in Hindi, English, and regional languages

### Benefits for Farmers
1. **Better price discovery:** Real-time prices from multiple mandis — farmers can compare and choose the best
2. **Transparent auction:** Online bidding by traders from across the country — no cartel/ring manipulation
3. **Direct buyer access:** Farmers can access buyers from any state, not just local traders
4. **Reduced intermediaries:** Direct linkage between farmer and buyer — higher farmer share of consumer price
5. **Online payment:** Payment via RTGS/NEFT directly to farmer's bank account — reduces cash dependency
6. **Quality assaying:** Mandatory quality testing at mandi — ensures fair price based on quality grade
7. **Single trading license:** Traders need only one eNAM license to trade across all connected mandis

### How to Register
1. **Visit nearest eNAM mandi** — approach the mandi secretary or eNAM help desk
2. **Documents:** Aadhaar card, bank account passbook, land records (or tenant certificate), mobile number, passport photo
3. **Online:** Visit enam.gov.in → "Farmer Registration" → fill details → get eNAM ID
4. **Through FPOs:** Farmer Producer Organizations can register their member farmers collectively
5. **Registration is FREE**

### How to Sell on eNAM
1. **Bring produce to eNAM-linked mandi** with gate entry slip
2. **Quality testing:** Produce tested at assaying lab in mandi (moisture, foreign matter, quality grade)
3. **Lot creation:** Mandi creates a lot with quality parameters visible to all traders
4. **Online bidding:** Traders from across India bid online; highest bid wins
5. **Acceptance:** Farmer can accept or reject the bid
6. **Weighment & payment:** Produce weighed, payment initiated through eNAM portal directly to farmer's bank account
7. **Receipt:** Digital receipt generated — can be used for crop loan/insurance records

### Mandis Connected
- **Total mandis integrated:** 1,389+ mandis across 23 states and 4 UTs (as of 2025)
- **Major states:** Uttar Pradesh (118), Madhya Pradesh (80), Rajasthan (144), Maharashtra (122), Gujarat (79), Haryana (54), Telangana (57), Andhra Pradesh (33), Karnataka (62), Tamil Nadu (63)
- **FPOs registered:** 2,000+ Farmer Producer Organizations

### Key Numbers
- **Portal:** enam.gov.in
- **Helpline:** **1800-270-0224** (toll-free)
- **Mobile App:** eNAM App (Google Play & iOS)

## 6. PM Kisan MaanDhan Yojana (Pension Scheme for Farmers)

### Overview
- **Launched:** September 2019
- **Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare
- **Objective:** Provide old age security and social protection to Small and Marginal Farmers (SMF) through a guaranteed monthly pension
- **Type:** Voluntary, contributory pension scheme (50:50 contribution by farmer and Central Government)
- **Fund managed by:** Life Insurance Corporation of India (LIC)

### Amount
- **Pension:** ₹3,000 per month (₹36,000 per year) guaranteed after the age of **60 years**
- **Family pension:** If farmer dies after 60, spouse receives 50% pension (₹1,500/month)
- **If farmer dies before 60:** Spouse can continue contributing and receive pension at 60, or exit with accumulated contribution + interest

### Eligibility
- **Age:** 18–40 years at the time of enrollment
- **Landholding:** Small and Marginal Farmers with cultivable land up to **2 hectares** (as per land records)
- **Not eligible:** Farmers covered under NPS, EPFO, or PM Shram Yogi Maandhan; Income Tax payers
- **PM-KISAN beneficiaries:** Can join and choose to auto-debit contribution from PM-KISAN installment

### Contribution (Monthly)
| Entry Age | Monthly Contribution (Farmer) | Govt Match |
|-----------|-------------------------------|------------|
| 18 years | ₹55 | ₹55 |
| 20 years | ₹61 | ₹61 |
| 25 years | ₹80 | ₹80 |
| 30 years | ₹105 | ₹105 |
| 35 years | ₹150 | ₹150 |
| 40 years | ₹200 | ₹200 |

- **Payment:** Monthly auto-debit from savings account or PM-KISAN installment

### How to Apply
1. **Visit nearest CSC** (Common Service Centre) with Aadhaar and bank passbook
2. **Online:** Visit maandhan.in → Select "Pradhan Mantri Kisan MaanDhan Yojana" → Self-enrollment using Aadhaar + mobile OTP
3. **Through PM-KISAN portal:** Option to link MaanDhan during PM-KISAN registration/update
4. **Documents:** Aadhaar card, savings bank account/Jan Dhan account, mobile number, land records
5. **Enrollment is FREE** — no registration charges

### Exit & Withdrawal Rules
- **Before 60 years (voluntary exit):** Farmer gets back only their contribution + savings bank interest
- **Default (stopped contributing):** Can resume or exit with accumulated amount
- **Superannuation (at 60):** Monthly pension of ₹3,000 for life

### Key Numbers
- **Portal:** maandhan.in
- **Helpline:** **14434** or **1800-267-6888** (toll-free)
- **Beneficiaries:** ~22 lakh farmers enrolled (as of 2025)

## 7. PKVY — Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (Organic Farming)

### Overview
- **Launched:** 2015 (under National Mission for Sustainable Agriculture — NMSA)
- **Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare
- **Objective:** Promote organic farming through cluster-based approach; support farmers in adoption of organic practices with financial assistance for inputs, certification, and marketing
- **Approach:** Cluster-based — groups of farmers form clusters for organic conversion
- **Certification:** PGS-India (Participatory Guarantee System) certification — peer-reviewed, low-cost organic certification

### Financial Assistance
- **₹50,000 per hectare** over 3 years (distributed as):
  - ₹31,000/ha — for organic inputs (bio-fertilizers, bio-pesticides, vermicompost, seeds)
  - ₹8,800/ha — for value addition, marketing, and packaging
  - ₹3,000/ha — for PGS certification and quality control
  - ₹7,200/ha — for cluster formation, capacity building, and exposure visits
- **Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT):** Funds transferred directly to farmer's Aadhaar-linked bank account
- **Conversion period:** 3 years support to transition from chemical to organic farming

### Eligibility
- **Cluster requirement:** Minimum **20 farmers** forming a group with at least **20 hectares** (50 acres) of contiguous or nearby land
- **Any farmer can join** — no land size restriction for individual farmers within a cluster
- **Priority:** Given to tribal areas, rainfed areas, and hilly/northeastern regions
- **Lead Resource Person (LRP):** Each cluster must have a trained farmer or NGO as lead

### How to Apply
1. **Form a cluster** of 20+ farmers with 20+ hectares
2. **Contact District Agriculture Officer** or State Agriculture Department
3. **Online portal:** pgsindia-ncof.gov.in — register cluster and individual farmers
4. **Through KVK:** Krishi Vigyan Kendras assist in cluster formation and training
5. **NGO/FPO route:** Many registered NGOs and FPOs facilitate PKVY cluster formation
6. **Approval:** District Agriculture Committee reviews and approves clusters

### PGS Certification Process
1. **Pledge:** Farmer signs pledge to follow organic practices
2. **Documentation:** Maintain farm diary (inputs used, practices followed)
3. **Peer inspection:** Fellow cluster farmers inspect each other's fields
4. **Local group certification:** Regional Council reviews and certifies
5. **Certification valid:** 1 year, renewable
6. **Cost to farmer:** FREE under PKVY (government covers certification cost)

### Key Numbers
- **Portal:** pgsindia-ncof.gov.in
- **Clusters formed:** 48,000+ clusters across India
- **Area covered:** ~7.5 lakh hectares under organic conversion

## 8. SMAM — Sub-Mission on Agricultural Mechanization

### Overview
- **Launched:** 2014-15 (under National Mission on Agricultural Extension & Technology)
- **Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare
- **Objective:** Increase the reach of farm mechanization to small and marginal farmers; promote Custom Hiring Centres (CHCs), Farm Machinery Banks, and hi-tech hubs
- **Budget (2025-26):** ~₹1,200 crore annually

### Subsidy Amount
| Category | Subsidy (% of cost) |
|----------|---------------------|
| Small & Marginal Farmers | **50%** |
| SC/ST/Women/NE Region Farmers | **50%** |
| Other/General Farmers | **40%** |
| Custom Hiring Centres (CHCs) | **40%** of project cost |
| Farm Machinery Banks (by FPOs/SHGs) | **80%** of project cost |
| Hi-Tech Hubs | **40%** of project cost |

### Equipment Covered
- **Land preparation:** Tractor (up to 20 HP for subsidy), power tiller, rotavator, MB plough, disc harrow, cultivator
- **Sowing & planting:** Seed drill, zero-till seed drill, paddy transplanter, sugarcane planter, seed-cum-fertilizer drill
- **Plant protection:** Power sprayer, knapsack sprayer, boom sprayer, drone sprayer (eligible since 2023)
- **Irrigation:** Drip/sprinkler components (under separate PMKSY), pump sets
- **Harvesting:** Reaper, combine harvester (small), thresher, groundnut digger
- **Post-harvest:** Chaff cutter, winnower, grain dryer, mini dal mill, mini oil expeller
- **Others:** Solar-powered equipment, mulch layer, raised bed planter, laser land leveler

### How to Apply
1. **Online portal:** Visit **agrimachinery.nic.in** → Register as farmer → Select equipment → Upload documents → Apply
2. **Documents needed:**
   - Aadhaar card
   - Land records (Khasra/Khatauni or lease deed)
   - Bank account details
   - Caste certificate (for SC/ST subsidy)
   - Quotation from authorized dealer
3. **Selection:** Applications processed on first-come-first-served basis (some states use lottery)
4. **DBT:** Subsidy amount credited directly to farmer's bank account after purchase verification
5. **Physical verification:** Agriculture department officer verifies equipment at farmer's location
6. **Timeline:** Typically 30–90 days from application to disbursement

### Key Numbers
- **Portal:** agrimachinery.nic.in
- **Helpline:** State agriculture department helplines
- **Beneficiaries:** Over 15 lakh farmers benefited (cumulative)

## 9. State-Specific Schemes

### Tamil Nadu
1. **Tamil Nadu State Crop Insurance:** Additional state-funded crop insurance beyond PMFBY for special crops (banana, coconut, etc.)
2. **Free Electricity for Agriculture:** Tamil Nadu provides free electricity to farmers for pump sets (up to 10 HP motor)
3. **Uzhavar Sandhai (Farmers' Market):** Direct-to-consumer markets eliminating middlemen — 180+ markets across the state
4. **Special Subsidy for Micro-Irrigation:** 100% subsidy for drip irrigation for small/marginal farmers (up to 5 acres)
5. **TN Irrigated Agriculture Modernization Project (IAMP):** World Bank-assisted modernization of irrigation systems

### Andhra Pradesh / Telangana
1. **Rythu Bandhu (Telangana):** ₹10,000 per acre per year (₹5,000 each for Kharif + Rabi season) — direct investment support for all land-owning farmers; no income/land size limit
2. **YSR Rythu Bharosa (Andhra Pradesh):** ₹13,500 per year per farmer family (₹7,500 state + ₹6,000 PM-KISAN) — combines state and central support
3. **Rythu Bima (Telangana):** Free life insurance of ₹5 lakh for all farmer families (age 18–59); premium paid entirely by state government
4. **YSR Free Crop Insurance (AP):** State pays farmer premium share of PMFBY — effectively free crop insurance for AP farmers
5. **E-Crop Registration:** Mandatory digital crop booking for accessing all scheme benefits

### Karnataka
1. **Raitha Siri:** Input subsidy for farmer groups practicing integrated farming
2. **Krishi Bhagya Scheme:** Farm ponds and polyethylene lining — 80% subsidy for rainwater harvesting (up to ₹1 lakh)
3. **Bhoochetana:** Soil health improvement program in partnership with ICRISAT — micronutrient supply
4. **Karnataka Raitha Suraksha Prashashthi:** ₹2 lakh insurance + ₹1 lakh accidental cover for registered farmers
5. **Free Electricity for Agriculture:** Up to 10 HP pump set — free power for farmers

### Maharashtra
1. **Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Shetkari Karj Mukti Yojana:** Farm loan waiver — up to ₹2 lakh per farmer (crop loans from cooperative banks)
2. **Gopinath Munde Shetkari Apghat Vima Yojana:** ₹2 lakh accidental death insurance + ₹1 lakh partial disability for farmers (age 10–75)
3. **Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Yojana:** World Bank-assisted climate-resilient agriculture — ₹4,000 crore project covering 5,142 villages
4. **Magel Tyala Shettale (Farm Pond):** 50–75% subsidy for farm ponds; priority for drought-prone areas
5. **Bhausaheb Fundkar Falbag Lagan Yojana:** Subsidy for fruit orchard plantation — ₹40,000–₹60,000/ha

### Punjab / Haryana
1. **Punjab — PM-KISAN + State Top-up:** Punjab provides additional benefits to PM-KISAN beneficiaries through state schemes
2. **Diversification of Paddy:** Incentive of ₹17,500/acre for farmers shifting from paddy to maize/cotton/other crops (to save groundwater)
3. **Mera Pani Meri Virasat (Haryana):** ₹7,000/acre incentive for farmers who switch from paddy to alternative crops; ₹2 lakh subsidy for micro-irrigation
4. **Free power for tube wells:** Both states provide highly subsidized/free electricity for agriculture
5. **Custom Hiring Centres:** State-subsidized tractor and harvester rental centres in every block
6. **Direct MSP Procurement:** Both states have robust government procurement system — over 95% of wheat and rice procured at MSP

### Uttar Pradesh
1. **UP Kisan Samman Nidhi:** State-level direct benefit scheme complementing PM-KISAN
2. **Kisan Rath App:** Government app for connecting farmers with transport vehicles during harvest season — reduces post-harvest losses
3. **Mukhyamantri Krishak Vriksh Dhan Yojana:** ₹30,000–₹50,000 per farmer for planting fruit trees on farmland (agroforestry incentive)
4. **UP Solar Pump Scheme:** 70% subsidy on solar water pumps (2 HP to 5 HP) for irrigation
5. **Pardarshi Kisan Seva Yojana:** Subsidized seeds, fertilizer distribution, and mini-kit program through single-window portal
6. **Free Boring Scheme:** Free boring of tube wells for SC/ST/small/marginal farmers (up to 70m depth)

### Odisha
1. **KALIA (Krushak Assistance for Livelihood and Income Augmentation):** ₹10,000 per family per year (₹5,000 each for Kharif + Rabi) for small/marginal farmers; ₹12,500/year for landless households
2. **Balaram Yojana:** Credit linkage for landless cultivators and sharecroppers — up to ₹1 lakh crop loan without collateral
3. **Free Crop Insurance:** Odisha pays PMFBY farmer premium from state funds

### Rajasthan
1. **Mukhyamantri Krishak Sathi Yojana:** ₹2 lakh assistance in case of farmer death/disability during farming operations
2. **Tarbandi Yojana:** Fencing subsidy — 50% or up to ₹48,000 for barbed wire fencing (400 running meters) to protect crops from stray animals
3. **Micro-Irrigation Subsidy:** 70% subsidy on drip/sprinkler systems for small farmers

### Kerala
1. **Haritashree:** State scheme for tree planting and agroforestry on farmlands — ₹50/seedling
2. **Subhiksha Keralam:** Food security scheme promoting rice, vegetable, tuber cultivation with input subsidies
3. **Jeevani:** Free crop insurance for paddy, banana, and pepper farmers

### Madhya Pradesh
1. **Bhavantar Bhugtan Yojana:** Price deficiency payment — if market price falls below MSP, government pays the difference directly to farmer
2. **Mukhyamantri Kisan Kalyan Yojana:** ₹4,000 per year on top of PM-KISAN (making total ₹10,000/year)
3. **Chief Minister Solar Pump Scheme:** 90% subsidy on solar pumps for farmers

## 10. Additional Central Government Schemes

### PMKSY — Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (Irrigation)
- **Launched:** 2015
- **Objective:** "Har Khet Ko Pani" — ensure irrigation access to every farm; "Per Drop More Crop" — promote micro-irrigation
- **Components:**
  - Accelerated Irrigation Benefits Programme (AIBP) — complete pending irrigation projects
  - Har Khet Ko Pani — water source creation, distribution, management
  - Per Drop More Crop — micro-irrigation (drip/sprinkler) with 55% subsidy (additional 10–20% from states)
  - Watershed Development
- **Subsidy:** 55% (Centre) + state share → farmer pays only 15–25% for drip/sprinkler systems
- **Apply:** Through state agriculture/horticulture departments; portal: pmksy.gov.in

### RKVY — Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana
- **Launched:** 2007, revamped as RKVY-RAFTAAR in 2017
- **Objective:** Incentivize states to increase public investment in agriculture; support agri-business startups and innovation
- **Funding:** ₹9,000+ crore annually (Centre:State = 60:40)
- **For farmers:** Infrastructure creation — cold storage, pack houses, seed processing, farm-to-market roads
- **Agri-startup component:** Up to ₹25 lakh for agriculture entrepreneurs/startups

### NFSM — National Food Security Mission
- **Objective:** Increase production of rice, wheat, pulses, coarse cereals, nutri-cereals, and oilseeds through area expansion, productivity enhancement, and technology demonstration
- **Support:** Subsidized certified seeds (50–100% subsidy), demonstration plots, improved technology adoption
- **Coverage:** All major food crop growing districts

### Micro Irrigation Fund (MIF)
- **Corpus:** ₹5,000 crore with NABARD
- **Purpose:** Provide states with affordable credit for expanding micro-irrigation infrastructure
- **Target:** 10 lakh hectares additional micro-irrigation coverage per year

### National Beekeeping & Honey Mission (NBHM)
- **Launched:** 2020 under Aatmanirbhar Bharat
- **Objective:** Promote scientific beekeeping, increase honey production, and provide additional income to farmers
- **Support:** Subsidy on beehive boxes, honey processing units, training
- **Benefit:** Additional income of ₹15,000–₹50,000 per year per farmer with 10–50 bee colonies

### PM-KUSUM — Pradhan Mantri Kisan Urja Suraksha evam Utthaan Mahabhiyan
- **Launched:** 2019
- **Objective:** Solar energy for farmers — reduce diesel/electricity dependence for irrigation
- **Components:**
  - **Component A:** 10,000 MW solar plants on barren/fallow land — farmers earn ₹60,000–₹1,00,000/year/MW by selling solar power
  - **Component B:** Standalone solar pumps (up to 7.5 HP) — 60% subsidy (30% Centre + 30% State); farmer pays 40%
  - **Component C:** Solarization of existing grid-connected pumps — sell surplus power to DISCOM
- **Apply:** Through state renewable energy agencies; portal: mnre.gov.in/kusum

### National Mission on Edible Oils — Oil Palm (NMEO-OP)
- **Launched:** 2021
- **Objective:** Increase domestic oilseed production; special focus on oil palm cultivation in NE India, A&N Islands
- **Support:** ₹29,000/ha for oil palm fresh fruit bunch (FFB) price assurance; ₹51,000/ha planting subsidy; input support for 4 years

## 15. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) — For AI Reference

**Q: I am a tenant farmer. Can I get PM-KISAN?**
A: PM-KISAN requires land in your name. However, some states have provisions for tenant farmers with proper documentation (lease deed registered with revenue department). Check with your state agriculture office.

**Q: My PM-KISAN installment is stuck. What do I do?**
A: Check eKYC status at pmkisan.gov.in → "Beneficiary Status." Common issues: Aadhaar not linked to bank, eKYC not done, land records mismatch. Visit CSC or agriculture office to resolve. Helpline: 155261.

**Q: Is crop insurance mandatory if I have a KCC loan?**
A: No. Since Kharif 2020, PMFBY is voluntary for all farmers, including loanee farmers with KCC. You can opt out by giving a written declaration to your bank before the cut-off date.

**Q: How do I report crop damage for insurance claim?**
A: Report within 72 hours via: (1) Crop Insurance App, (2) call 14447, (3) inform your bank or insurance company, (4) contact local agriculture officer. Take photos of damaged crop.

**Q: What is the interest rate on KCC loan?**
A: 7% per annum. With interest subvention: 4% effective rate for loans up to ₹3 lakh repaid within 1 year. Some states provide additional subvention making it as low as 2%.

**Q: I want to switch to organic farming. What help can I get?**
A: Apply for PKVY through your district agriculture office. You need a cluster of 20+ farmers with 20+ hectares. You get ₹50,000/ha over 3 years + free PGS certification. Also explore state organic missions.

**Q: How can I get a solar pump for my farm?**
A: Apply under PM-KUSUM Component B through your state renewable energy agency. You get 60% subsidy (30% Centre + 30% State). Standalone solar pumps up to 7.5 HP available. Check mnre.gov.in/kusum.

**Q: Which scheme gives the highest direct money?**
A: Rythu Bandhu (Telangana) gives ₹10,000/acre/year. PM-KISAN gives ₹6,000/year nationally. Combined with state schemes (like MP's ₹4,000 top-up), farmers can receive ₹10,000–₹16,000/year in direct transfers.

**Q: I want to buy a tractor. Is there subsidy?**
A: Under SMAM, tractors up to 20 HP get 40–50% subsidy. For larger tractors, check state-specific schemes. Apply at agrimachinery.nic.in with land records, Aadhaar, and dealer quotation.

**Q: I want to sell my produce at better prices. How?**
A: Register on eNAM (enam.gov.in) for free. Bring your produce to the nearest eNAM-linked mandi. Traders from across India bid online — you get the best price. Payment directly to your bank account.
