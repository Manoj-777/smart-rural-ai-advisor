## 1. Rice (Paddy) — ✅ EXAMPLE (fully filled)

**Botanical Name:** Oryza sativa
**Crop Type:** Cereal / Kharif
**Growing Season:** June–November (Kharif), January–May (Rabi in some regions)
**Duration:** 120-150 days

### Soil Requirements
- **Ideal Soil:** Clay loam, silty clay — water-retentive soils preferred
- **pH Range:** 5.5–6.5
- **Drainage:** Poor drainage acceptable (paddy needs standing water)

### Climate & Water
- **Temperature:** 20–35°C (optimal 25–30°C)
- **Rainfall:** 1000–2000 mm annually
- **Water Need:** 1200–2000 mm per crop cycle (most water-intensive cereal)
- **Irrigation:** Flood irrigation traditional; SRI method reduces water by 40%

### Regions in India
- **Major States:** West Bengal, Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Odisha, Bihar
- **Best Suited:** Alluvial plains, river deltas, coastal lowlands

### Planting Guide
- **Seed Rate:** 20–25 kg/hectare (transplanted), 80–100 kg/hectare (direct seeded)
- **Spacing:** 20cm × 15cm (transplanting)
- **Nursery Duration:** 21–25 days before transplanting

### Yield
- **Average Yield:** 3.5–5 tonnes/hectare (irrigated)
- **High-Yield Varieties:** Pusa Basmati 1121, IR-64, Swarna, BPT-5204 (Samba Masuri)
- **Harvest Indicator:** Grains turn golden, 80% panicles mature

### Key Nutrients
- **NPK Requirement:** 120:60:40 kg/hectare
- **Micronutrients:** Zinc (ZnSO4 25 kg/ha), Iron in acidic soils

### Market Info
- **MSP (2025-26):** ₹2,300/quintal (paddy)
- **Major Markets:** APMC mandis, FCI procurement, direct mill purchase

### Common Diseases
- Blast (Pyricularia oryzae), Brown spot, Bacterial leaf blight, Sheath blight

### Traditional Wisdom
- In Tamil Nadu, rice is traditionally planted during "Aadi Pattam" (mid-July)
- Companion crop: Azolla (nitrogen fixer) grown alongside paddy
