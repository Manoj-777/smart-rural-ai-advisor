## 1. Tamil Nadu

### Geography & Climate
- **Agro-climatic zones:** 7 zones — Cauvery Delta (alluvial, irrigated rice), North-Eastern (coastal), North-Western (dry farming), Western (hilly, plantation crops), Southern (rainfed, dry millets), High Rainfall Zone, Hilly & Tribal Zone (Nilgiris)
- **Rainfall:** Annual average ~945 mm. North-East monsoon (Oct–Dec) contributes ~48% of rainfall; SW monsoon (Jun–Sep) contributes ~35%. Cauvery Delta receives 1,000–1,200 mm; southern districts 600–800 mm
- **Temperature range:** Coastal: 24–37°C; Interior plains: 20–40°C; Nilgiris: 5–20°C
- **Major soil types:** Red soil (45% area), Black cotton soil (Thanjavur, Ramanathapuram), Alluvial soil (Cauvery Delta), Laterite (Western Ghats foothills), Sandy coastal soil (East coast)

### Major Crops by Season
| Season | Months | Major Crops |
|---|---|---|
| Kharif (Samba) | Jun–Jan | Rice (ADT-43, CO-51, BPT-5204), Cotton, Groundnut, Millets (Ragi, Bajra), Sugarcane |
| Rabi (Navarai) | Feb–May | Rice (short duration), Pulses (Black gram, Green gram), Sesame, Sunflower |
| Summer (Kuruvai) | Apr–Aug | Rice (short duration), Vegetables, Watermelon, Gourds |

### Key Districts & Specialties
- **Thanjavur / Tiruvarur / Nagapattinam:** "Rice Bowl of Tamil Nadu" — Samba and Ponni rice varieties, 3 crops/year in delta areas
- **Coimbatore:** Coconut, Turmeric (Erode variety famous globally), Cotton, Sugarcane
- **Madurai / Virudhunagar:** Jasmine (Madurai Malli — GI tag), Banana (Nendran), Chillies
- **Dindigul:** Cardamom, Pepper, Hill banana
- **Nilgiris:** Tea (12,000+ ha), Carrots, Potato, Cabbage, Garlic, Eucalyptus
- **Krishnagiri / Dharmapuri:** Mango (Alphonso, Banganapalli), Tamarind, Tomato
- **Kanyakumari:** Rubber, Clove, Nutmeg

### Current Challenges
- **Cauvery water dispute:** Ongoing inter-state water sharing conflict with Karnataka; delta farmers face irrigation uncertainty
- **Monsoon variability:** NE monsoon failure in recent years causing drought; 2023-24 saw 20% deficit rainfall
- **Groundwater depletion:** Over-exploitation in western and southern TN; water table dropped 3–5 m in Coimbatore, Erode districts
- **Soil salinity:** Coastal areas (Nagapattinam, Cuddalore) face salinity intrusion
- **Pest pressure:** Fall armyworm in maize; Brown planthopper in rice

### Govt Schemes Specific to TN
- **TNIAMP (Tamil Nadu Irrigated Agriculture Modernization Project):** ₹2,962 crore World Bank-aided project for irrigation modernization
- **Free Electricity for Farmers:** Free electricity for agricultural pumpsets
- **Crop Insurance:** State-funded PMFBY top-up; additional coverage for horticultural crops
- **Uzhavar Sandhai:** Direct farmer-to-consumer markets in 120+ locations
- **TN Precision Farming Project:** Drip/sprinkler irrigation subsidies up to 100% for small farmers

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Rice (Navarai):** Begin nursery preparation; use short-duration varieties (ADT-43, CO-51); apply basal dose of DAP 100 kg/ha
- **Pulses:** Ideal time for Black gram and Green gram sowing in rainfed areas; seed treatment with Rhizobium culture
- **Summer vegetables:** Transplant tomato, brinjal, chilli seedlings; provide drip irrigation; mulch to conserve moisture
- **Mango:** Flowering stage — spray 2% KNO₃ for uniform flowering; watch for mango hopper (spray Imidacloprid 0.3 ml/L)
- **General:** Complete Rabi harvest before onset of summer heat; prepare land for Kuruvai rice; clean irrigation channels

## 2. Andhra Pradesh

### Geography & Climate
- **Agro-climatic zones:** 7 zones — Krishna-Godavari Zone (alluvial, irrigated), Scarce Rainfall Zone (Rayalaseema — Anantapur, Kurnool), Southern Zone, North Coastal Zone, High Altitude Zone (Eastern Ghats)
- **Rainfall:** Annual average ~940 mm. SW monsoon (Jun–Sep) contributes ~65%; NE monsoon (Oct–Dec) ~25%. Coastal districts: 1,000–1,200 mm; Rayalaseema: 550–700 mm
- **Temperature range:** Coastal: 22–38°C; Rayalaseema: 18–45°C (extreme summers); Eastern Ghats: 10–28°C
- **Major soil types:** Black cotton soil (35% — Krishna, Guntur, Prakasam), Red soil (35% — Rayalaseema), Alluvial soil (20% — Godavari, Krishna deltas), Laterite (Agency areas), Sandy loam (coastal)

### Major Crops by Season
| Season | Months | Major Crops |
|---|---|---|
| Kharif | Jun–Oct | Rice (BPT-5204, MTU-1010, NLR-34449), Cotton (Bt), Maize, Groundnut, Red gram (Pigeon pea), Chilli |
| Rabi | Nov–Mar | Rice (Godavari delta), Bengal gram (Chickpea), Sunflower, Tobacco (Guntur), Jowar, Black gram |
| Summer | Mar–May | Rice (3rd crop in deltas), Vegetables, Watermelon, Sesame |

### Key Districts & Specialties
- **East Godavari / West Godavari:** "Rice granary of AP" — 3 crops/year; Aquaculture (shrimp — ₹30,000 crore industry); Coconut, Oil Palm
- **Guntur:** Chilli capital of India (Guntur Sannam — GI tag); Tobacco (Virginia flue-cured)
- **Kurnool / Anantapur:** Groundnut belt (largest in India); Sunflower
- **Krishna / Prakasam:** Cotton, Pulses, Aquaculture
- **Chittoor:** Mango (Banganapalli, Totapuri — exports to 30+ countries), Tomato, Sugarcane
- **Srikakulam / Vizianagaram:** Cashew nut (AP is 2nd largest producer)
- **Alluri Sitharama Raju district (Agency areas):** Coffee, Pepper, Turmeric, Tribal farming

### Current Challenges
- **Cyclone vulnerability:** East coast prone to severe cyclones (Hudhud 2014, Titli 2018, Michaung 2023); crop damage ₹5,000–10,000 crore per event
- **Groundnut price volatility:** Anantapur farmers face frequent crop failure (only 550 mm rainfall) and market crashes
- **Aquaculture disease:** White spot syndrome in shrimp; antibiotic overuse concerns
- **Soil degradation:** Over-irrigation in delta areas causing waterlogging; Fluoride contamination in Nalgonda belt
- **Water scarcity in Rayalaseema:** Despite Penna and Tungabhadra rivers, chronic drought in Anantapur (declared drought-prone district)

### State-Specific Schemes
- **YSR Rythu Bharosa:** ₹13,500/year per farmer family (₹7,500 Kharif + ₹4,000 Rabi + ₹2,000 PM-Kisan)
- **YSR Free Crop Insurance:** State-funded; zero premium for farmers; covers all major crops
- **Dr. YSR Horticultural University:** Free saplings, technical guidance for horticulture
- **AP Micro Irrigation Project:** 90% subsidy for drip/sprinkler for small and marginal farmers
- **Rythu Bazaars:** Direct marketing; 100+ functional markets

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Rabi rice harvest:** Complete harvesting before March heat; avoid field burning of stubble — use Happy Seeder or incorporate residue
- **Summer rice nursery:** Begin nursery for 3rd crop in Godavari delta; use MTU-1010 or MTU-1001 (short duration, 120 days)
- **Chilli (Guntur):** Harvesting and drying season; ensure proper sun-drying on tarpaulin sheets for quality; grade and store in gunny bags
- **Mango:** Flowering to fruit-set stage; spray Imidacloprid for hoppers; apply Bordeaux mixture for anthracnose
- **Groundnut:** Land preparation in Anantapur for pre-monsoon sowing; treat seed with Trichoderma viride @ 4 g/kg seed
- **General:** Prepare compost pits; apply FYM 10 t/ha to fields before summer ploughing

## 3. Telangana

### Geography & Climate
- **Agro-climatic zones:** 3 zones — Northern Telangana Zone (higher rainfall, red soil), Central Telangana Zone (mixed, black + red soil), Southern Telangana Zone (drier, Mahabubnagar)
- **Rainfall:** Annual average ~900 mm. SW monsoon (Jun–Sep) provides ~80% rainfall. Northern Telangana: 1,000–1,100 mm; Southern: 650–750 mm
- **Temperature range:** 15–42°C; Adilabad: 12–42°C; Hyderabad: 18–40°C
- **Major soil types:** Red sandy loam (50%), Black cotton soil (30% — Karimnagar, Warangal, Nalgonda), Laterite (Adilabad), Alluvial (along Godavari, Krishna)

### Major Crops by Season
| Season | Months | Major Crops |
|---|---|---|
| Kharif | Jun–Oct | Rice (BPT-5204, Tellahamsa, RNR-15048), Cotton (Bt), Maize, Soybean, Red gram, Chilli |
| Rabi | Nov–Mar | Rice (irrigated), Bengal gram (Chickpea), Groundnut, Jowar, Sunflower, Maize |
| Summer | Mar–May | Vegetables, Watermelon, Muskmelon, Sesame |

### Key Districts & Specialties
- **Karimnagar / Rajanna Sircilla:** Rice, Turmeric (Nizamabad turmeric — 2nd largest producer in India)
- **Warangal / Jangaon:** Cotton (Bt cotton hub of Telangana), Chilli
- **Nalgonda / Suryapet:** Rice (canal-irrigated from Nagarjuna Sagar), Pulses
- **Medak / Sangareddy:** Maize, Poultry (India's 2nd largest poultry hub), Dairy
- **Adilabad / Nirmal:** Soybean, Cotton, Tribal farming (millets revival)
- **Mahabubnagar / Nagarkurnool:** Jowar, Castor, Sheep rearing (Deccani breed)
- **Rangareddy / Hyderabad peri-urban:** Floriculture (roses, marigold), Vegetables, Dairy

### Current Challenges
- **Cotton monoculture:** Over-dependence on Bt cotton in Warangal/Adilabad; farmer suicides linked to crop failure and debt
- **Fluoride in groundwater:** Nalgonda, Warangal districts — affects drinking water and irrigation quality
- **Declining soil fertility:** Continuous rice-rice or rice-maize without legume rotation; organic carbon <0.5%
- **Tank irrigation neglect:** 46,000+ minor irrigation tanks in disrepair; Mission Kakatiya only partially restored them
- **Pest resistance:** Bollworm resistance to Bt cotton; pink bollworm emergence

### State-Specific Schemes
- **Rythu Bandhu:** ₹10,000/acre/year (₹5,000 per season) — direct investment support to land-owning farmers
- **Rythu Bima:** Free life insurance (₹5 lakh) for farmers aged 18–59
- **Mission Kakatiya:** Restoration of minor irrigation tanks; ₹22,000+ crore investment; 46,000 tanks targeted
- **24×7 Free Power:** Uninterrupted free electricity for agriculture pumpsets
- **Telangana Horticulture Mission:** Subsidies for mango, citrus, oil palm plantations

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Rabi rice:** Complete harvesting; avoid delays to prevent grain discoloration
- **Bengal gram:** Pod filling to maturity stage; watch for Helicoverpa pod borer (spray Chlorantraniliprole 0.3 ml/L if >2 larvae/m row)
- **Cotton:** Land preparation for next Kharif; deep summer ploughing to expose soil pests; apply FYM 5 t/acre
- **Turmeric (Nizamabad):** Harvesting season; cure properly for 40–45 days; boil, dry, and polish for market
- **Mango:** Spray monocrotophos alternative (Thiamethoxam 0.2 g/L) for hopper control; bagging of fruits for premium grade
- **General:** Plan crop rotation — include pulses (green gram, black gram) in Kharif to restore soil nitrogen

## 4. Karnataka

### Geography & Climate
- **Agro-climatic zones:** 10 zones — Coastal Zone (heavy rainfall), Northern Dry Zone (Bijapur, Raichur — arid), Northern Transition Zone, Central Dry Zone (Chitradurga, Tumkur), Eastern Dry Zone (Bangalore rural), Southern Dry Zone, Southern Transition Zone, Hilly Zone (Kodagu, Chikmagalur), North-East Dry Zone, North-East Transition Zone
- **Rainfall:** Annual average ~1,139 mm. Western Ghats districts: 3,000–5,000 mm; North Karnataka (Bijapur): 500–600 mm; Kodagu: 3,500 mm
- **Temperature range:** Coastal: 22–34°C; North Karnataka: 15–44°C (extreme); Malnad (hilly): 12–28°C
- **Major soil types:** Red soil (55%), Black cotton soil (25% — North Karnataka), Laterite (coastal, Malnad), Alluvial (river basins), Sandy (coastal)

### Major Crops by Season
| Season | Months | Major Crops |
|---|---|---|
| Kharif | Jun–Oct | Rice (Jaya, IR-64), Ragi (Finger millet), Maize, Cotton, Tur dal (Red gram), Jowar, Sugarcane, Groundnut |
| Rabi | Nov–Mar | Jowar (North Karnataka), Bengal gram, Sunflower, Wheat (Bidar, Dharwad), Safflower |
| Summer/Perennial | Year-round | Coffee, Pepper, Cardamom, Arecanut, Coconut, Silk (mulberry) |

### Key Districts & Specialties
- **Kodagu (Coorg):** Coffee (India's largest producer state — 71% of national production; Robusta & Arabica), Pepper, Cardamom, Orange
- **Mysuru / Mandya:** Sugarcane (KSE mills), Silk (Mysore Silk — GI tag; India's 40% raw silk), Ragi, Rice
- **Hassan:** Coffee, Coconut, Arecanut, Potato (Sakleshpura)
- **Belgaum (Belagavi):** Sugarcane (largest production district in Karnataka), Jowar, Tobacco
- **Bijapur / Bagalkot:** Grapes (Bijapur grapes — export quality), Pomegranate, Jowar, Onion
- **Raichur / Bellary:** Rice (Tungabhadra command area), Cotton, Sunflower
- **Shimoga / Chikmagalur:** Arecanut (India's #1 producer state), Paddy, Pepper
- **Tumkur:** Coconut capital of Karnataka, Groundnut, Ragi

### Current Challenges
- **North Karnataka drought:** Persistent water scarcity in Bijapur, Raichur, Gulbarga; farmer distress high
- **Cauvery dispute:** Inter-state water sharing with Tamil Nadu affects Mandya, Mysuru irrigation
- **Coffee price volatility:** Global market fluctuations affect Kodagu/Chikmagalur livelihoods; White Stem Borer pest increasing
- **Sugarcane arrears:** Delayed payments from sugar mills causing farmer protests
- **Soil degradation:** Red soil areas losing topsoil; organic carbon depletion in intensive farming zones

### State-Specific Schemes
- **Raitha Siri:** Financial assistance to farmers for land development and inputs
- **Krishi Bhagya Scheme:** Farm ponds for rainwater harvesting; 80% subsidy
- **Bhoochetana:** Soil health improvement program by ICRISAT partnership; covered 5 million hectares
- **Karnataka Horticulture Mission:** Subsidies for fruit orchards, spice plantations
- **Free Crop Insurance (State share):** Top-up on PMFBY for cereals, pulses, and oilseeds

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Coffee:** Berry development stage; apply 2nd dose of fertilizer (NPK 40:30:30 kg/ha); shade management critical
- **Ragi harvest:** Complete by mid-February; thresh and store at <12% moisture
- **Sugarcane:** Planting season in Belagavi, Mandya; use sets treated with carbendazim; plant in 90 cm rows
- **Grapes (Bijapur):** Pruning for 2nd harvest (Hasta Bahar); apply Gibberellic acid for berry size
- **Mango:** Flowering stage in south Karnataka; spray Imidacloprid for hopper; avoid chemical spray during full bloom (kills pollinators)
- **Summer ploughing:** Essential in North Karnataka — expose Helicoverpa pupae; conserve soil moisture for Kharif

## 5. Maharashtra

### Geography & Climate
- **Agro-climatic zones:** 9 zones — Konkan Coastal (heavy rain), Western Ghat Zone, Transition Zone, Scarcity Zone (Marathwada — drought-prone), Assured Rainfall Zone, Moderate Rainfall Zone, Eastern Vidarbha, Western Vidarbha, and Sub-montane Zone
- **Rainfall:** Annual average ~1,100 mm. Konkan: 2,500–4,000 mm; Western Maharashtra: 600–800 mm; Marathwada: 650–750 mm (highly erratic); Vidarbha: 800–1,100 mm
- **Temperature range:** Konkan: 22–35°C; Vidarbha (Nagpur): 12–48°C (India's hottest zone); Pune plateau: 10–38°C
- **Major soil types:** Black cotton soil (Deccan trap — 65% area; deep in Vidarbha, medium in Marathwada, shallow in W. Maharashtra), Laterite (Konkan), Red soil (Chandrapur), Alluvial (river valleys)

### Major Crops by Season
| Season | Months | Major Crops |
|---|---|---|
| Kharif | Jun–Oct | Soybean (Vidarbha/Marathwada — India's #1), Cotton (Bt — Vidarbha, Khandesh), Rice (Konkan), Jowar, Tur dal, Bajra, Sugarcane |
| Rabi | Nov–Mar | Wheat (Nashik, Pune), Jowar (drought-hardy), Bengal gram (Chickpea), Onion (Nashik, Pune), Safflower |
| Perennial/Summer | Year-round | Sugarcane (W. Maharashtra), Grapes (Nashik — 80% of India's grapes), Pomegranate (Solapur), Banana (Jalgaon), Orange (Nagpur) |

### Key Districts & Specialties
- **Nashik:** Grapes (Nashik grapes — export quality, wine production), Onion (India's #1 producer district), Tomato, Pomegranate
- **Kolhapur / Sangli / Satara:** Sugarcane belt (65+ cooperative sugar mills), Turmeric (Sangli), Dairy (Kolhapur — Gokul Dairy)
- **Nagpur:** Oranges (Nagpur Orange — GI tag; 30% of India's citrus), Cotton, Soybean
- **Vidarbha (Amravati, Yavatmal, Akola, Washim):** Cotton, Soybean, Tur — severe agrarian crisis zone
- **Jalgaon:** Banana capital of India (Jalgaon banana — tissue culture, export to Middle East)
- **Solapur / Osmanabad:** Pomegranate (India's 70% production), Jowar, Onion
- **Konkan (Ratnagiri, Sindhudurg):** Alphonso Mango (Ratnagiri Hapus — GI tag), Cashew, Kokum, Rice

### Current Challenges
- **Farmer distress in Vidarbha & Marathwada:** Highest farmer suicide rates in India; linked to cotton crop failure, debt from Bt cotton seeds + pesticides
- **Water scarcity in Marathwada:** Jayakwadi Dam rarely at full capacity; tanker-dependent villages; successive droughts
- **Onion price volatility:** Extreme price swings (₹2/kg to ₹100/kg) devastate Nashik farmers
- **Sugarcane water footprint:** Sugarcane uses 60% of Maharashtra's irrigation water but covers only 4% of cropped area
- **Hailstorms:** Increasing unseasonal hail (March) damaging grapes, wheat, and mango

### State-Specific Schemes
- **Mahatma Jyotirao Phule Shetkari Karz Mukti Yojana:** Farm loan waiver scheme (up to ₹2 lakh)
- **Nanaji Deshmukh Krishi Sanjivani Yojana (PoCRA):** Climate-resilient agriculture project (World Bank — ₹4,000 crore) in Marathwada/Vidarbha
- **Magel Tyala Shettale:** Farm pond scheme — 50% subsidy on farm ponds
- **Bhausaheb Fundkar Horticulture Scheme:** Subsidies for fruit orchards (mango, pomegranate, citrus)
- **Chief Minister's Solar Pump Scheme:** 100,000 solar pumps distributed; 95% subsidy

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Onion (Nashik):** Late Kharif harvest ongoing; store in well-ventilated structures (Nashik-type onion storage); avoid bruising during handling
- **Wheat (Pune, Nashik):** Grain filling stage; apply one light irrigation; watch for rust — spray Propiconazole 1 ml/L if yellow spots appear
- **Grapes (Nashik):** Harvest and packing for export; maintain cold chain at 0–2°C; SO₂ pad for extended shelf life
- **Sugarcane:** Planting season (Suru); use 3-bud setts treated with Carbendazim; plant at 120 cm row spacing
- **Mango (Konkan):** Flowering to fruit-set; spray Hexaconazole for powdery mildew; avoid excessive nitrogen
- **Summer ploughing:** Critical in Vidarbha — break soil hard pan; expose cotton bollworm pupae

## 6. Punjab

### Geography & Climate
- **Agro-climatic zones:** 3 zones — Sub-Mountain Undulating Zone (Pathankot, Hoshiarpur — foothills), Central Plain Zone (Ludhiana, Jalandhar — prime farmland), Western Zone (Bathinda, Fazilka — arid)
- **Rainfall:** Annual average ~650 mm. SW monsoon (Jul–Sep) brings 70–80% rainfall. Sub-mountain: 900–1,100 mm; Western: 350–450 mm (desert fringe)
- **Temperature range:** Summer: 25–47°C (Bathinda can exceed 47°C); Winter: 0–20°C (frost common Nov–Feb in central Punjab)
- **Major soil types:** Alluvial soil (95% area — highly fertile Indo-Gangetic plain), Sandy loam (western zone), Loamy (central), Clay loam (sub-mountain)

### Major Crops by Season
| Season | Months | Major Crops |
|---|---|---|
| Kharif | Jun–Oct | Rice (Pusa-44, PR-126, Basmati — 1509, 1121), Maize (Hoshiarpur), Cotton (Bathinda, Fazilka), Sugarcane |
| Rabi | Nov–Apr | Wheat (HD-3086, PBW-725, HD-2967) — India's bread basket, Mustard, Barley, Potato (Jalandhar), Peas |
| Summer/Zaid | Apr–Jun | Moong (Green gram), Fodder crops (Maize, Jowar), Vegetables (Cucurbits, Okra) |

### Key Districts & Specialties
- **Ludhiana:** Wheat-rice heartland; PAU (Punjab Agricultural University) — India's premier agricultural university; Dairy
- **Amritsar / Tarn Taran:** Wheat, Rice (Basmati belt); Food processing (pickles, papad)
- **Jalandhar / Kapurthala:** Potato (25% of India's seed potato), Vegetables, Dairy
- **Bathinda / Mansa / Fazilka:** Cotton belt of Punjab; Kinnow (mandarin orange — 2nd largest after Maharashtra citrus), Guar
- **Hoshiarpur / Ropar:** Maize, Citrus (Kinnow), Sub-tropical fruits, Bee-keeping
- **Sangrur / Patiala:** Wheat, Rice; Largest food grain producing belt

### Current Challenges
- **Groundwater crisis:** Water table falling 0.5–1 m/year; 79% of Punjab's blocks are "over-exploited"; Central Ground Water Board classifies Punjab as "critical"
- **Stubble burning:** 15–20 million tonnes of paddy straw burned annually; contributes 30–40% of Delhi's winter air pollution; despite ₹2,500/acre incentive for non-burning
- **Wheat-rice monoculture:** Same rotation for 50+ years; declining soil organic matter; micronutrient deficiency (Zinc, Iron, Manganese)
- **Excessive chemical use:** Fertilizer consumption at 223 kg/ha (national avg: 135 kg/ha); pesticide use 923 g/ha (national: 305 g/ha); cancer belt in Malwa (Bathinda, Mansa)
- **Declining farm size:** Average holding 3.6 ha but shrinking; 34% are small/marginal farmers
- **Paddy variety issue:** Pusa-44 takes 160 days, delays wheat sowing; government promoting PR-126 (125 days)

### State-Specific Schemes
- **Punjab Crop Residue Management:** ₹2,500/acre incentive for in-situ management; subsidized Happy Seeders and Super SMS machines
- **Punjab Kisan Credit Card:** Subsidized credit at 4% interest
- **Diversification of Punjab Agriculture:** Government promotion of Maize, Pulses, Cotton, Horticulture to reduce rice area
- **PAU-recommended packages:** Free soil testing and variety recommendation through KVKs
- **PM-KUSUM:** Solar pump adoption for replacing diesel/electric tube wells

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Wheat:** Critical grain-filling stage; apply 3rd irrigation at milking stage (85–90 DAS); watch for Yellow Rust — spray Propiconazole 25EC @ 1 ml/L if pustules appear on leaves
- **Mustard:** Pod-filling stage; apply one irrigation; Aphid monitoring — if >25 aphids/plant, spray Dimethoate 30EC @ 1 ml/L
- **Potato (Jalandhar):** Harvest season (Feb–Mar); cure in shade for 10 days before cold storage; avoid harvesting in wet conditions
- **Sugarcane:** Spring planting (Feb–Mar); use CoPb-91, CoPb-94 varieties; sett treatment with Carbendazim + Malathion
- **Summer Moong:** Prepare land after wheat harvest (April); use SML-668 or IPM-02-3 varieties — excellent for restoring soil nitrogen
- **General:** Book/maintain Happy Seeder for next Kharif to manage paddy straw; service tube wells before summer demand peak

## 7. Uttar Pradesh

### Geography & Climate
- **Agro-climatic zones:** 9 zones — Bhabar & Tarai (Sub-Himalayan), Western Plain, Mid-Western Plain, South-Western Semi-Arid, Central Plain, Bundelkhand, North-Eastern Plain, Eastern Plain, Vindhyan Zone
- **Rainfall:** Annual average ~990 mm. Western UP: 600–800 mm; Eastern UP: 1,000–1,200 mm; Bundelkhand: 800–900 mm (erratic)
- **Temperature range:** Winter: 3–20°C (severe frost in Tarai); Summer: 30–47°C (Bundelkhand extreme heat)
- **Major soil types:** Alluvial (85% — Indo-Gangetic plain; both Old Alluvium/Bangar and New Alluvium/Khadar), Red soil (Bundelkhand), Sandy (Western UP near Rajasthan), Usar/Sodic soil (7 lakh ha in central UP)

### Major Crops by Season
| Season | Months | Major Crops |
|---|---|---|
| Kharif | Jun–Oct | Rice (most produced crop), Sugarcane (12-month), Maize, Bajra, Arhar (Tur dal), Soybean, Vegetables |
| Rabi | Nov–Apr | Wheat (UP is India's #1 producer), Mustard, Potato (India's #1), Barley, Peas, Lentil (Masoor), Gram |
| Summer/Zaid | Apr–Jun | Watermelon, Muskmelon, Moong, Cucumber, Fodder |

### Key Districts & Specialties
- **Lucknow / Barabanki:** Mango (Dasheri), Sugarcane, Floriculture
- **Agra / Mathura:** Potato, Wheat, Dairy (Mathura pedha)
- **Meerut / Muzaffarnagar:** Sugarcane belt (India's #1 sugar producing state — 30+ mills), Wheat, Jaggery
- **Varanasi / Jaunpur:** Rice, Vegetables, Betel leaf (Banarasi paan)
- **Gorakhpur / Deoria:** Rice (Kalanamak — aromatic GI-tagged rice), Fish, Banana
- **Allahabad (Prayagraj):** Guava (Allahabad Safeda — GI tag), Amla (Indian Gooseberry)
- **Bundelkhand (Jhansi, Banda, Chitrakoot):** Drought-prone; Ber, Aonla, Sesame, Lentil
- **Farrukhabad / Kannauj:** Potato, Perfumery (Kannauj — attar/essential oils from flowers)

### Current Challenges
- **Sugarcane arrears:** Pending payments of ₹10,000+ crore to farmers from sugar mills
- **Sodic/Usar soil:** 7+ lakh hectares unproductive due to alkalinity; UP Bhumi Sudhar Nigam working on reclamation with gypsum
- **Flood-drought cycle:** Eastern UP (Gorakhpur, Ballia) faces annual flooding; Bundelkhand faces chronic drought
- **Groundwater depletion:** Western UP (Meerut, Agra) water table dropping; canal water distribution uneven
- **Small landholdings:** Average farm size 0.73 ha (smallest among major states); limits mechanization

### State-Specific Schemes
- **UP Kisan Samman Nidhi:** State top-up of ₹6,000/year over PM-KISAN
- **Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana:** Organic farming clusters — 50 farmers/cluster
- **UP Sugar Industry Policy:** Support for mill modernization; ethanol blending promotion
- **Mukhya Mantri Krishak Durghatna Kalyan Yojana:** ₹5 lakh insurance for farmer accidents
- **UP Horticulture Mission:** Subsidies for mango, guava, banana plantations; integrated cold chains

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Wheat:** Grain filling stage; apply final irrigation (4th or 5th) at dough stage; monitor for Karnal Bunt in Eastern UP
- **Mustard:** Pod maturity and harvest; harvest when 75% pods turn brown; thresh within 3 days of cutting

| Kharif | Jun–Oct | Rice (wet rice in valleys, jhum rice on hills), Maize, Ginger, Turmeric, Vegetables |
| Rabi | Nov–Mar | Mustard, Potato, Vegetables (cabbage, pea, beans), Rapeseed |
| Perennial | Year-round | Large Cardamom (Sikkim & Arunachal), Orange (Khasi mandarin), Pineapple, Passion fruit, Kiwi, Black Pepper, Bay leaf, Broom grass |

### State-Wise Specialties
- **Meghalaya:** Strawberry (Shillong), Turmeric (Lakadong — highest curcumin content in world, GI tag potential), Orange, Pineapple, Arecanut
- **Mizoram:** Passion fruit (brand "Zawlbuk"), Bird's eye chilli, Anthurium flowers, Ginger, Turmeric
- **Nagaland:** King Chilli (Bhut Jolokia / Raja Mirchi — GI tag; one of world's hottest), Orange, Tree tomato, Naga cucumber
- **Manipur:** Black rice (Chak-Hao — GI tag, superfood), Pineapple, Ginger, Kachai lemon (GI tag)
- **Arunachal Pradesh:** Large Cardamom (India's 2nd after Sikkim), Kiwi (Ziro valley), Orange, Apple (Tawang), Walnut

### Current Challenges
- **Shifting cultivation (Jhum):** Dominant practice in Nagaland, Mizoram, Manipur hills — cycle shortened from 15–20 years to 3–5 years; soil degradation severe
- **Connectivity & markets:** Poor infrastructure; perishables lose 30–40% before reaching market
- **Acidic soil:** All states have strongly acidic soils requiring regular liming
- **Insurgency/instability:** Some areas have limited government extension services
- **Landslides:** Monsoon-triggered landslides damage farms and block supply chains

### Govt Schemes (NE Region)
- **Mission Organic Value Chain Development for NE (MOVCDNER):** ₹400 crore for organic farming clusters
- **North East Initiative for Horticulture (NEIH):** Promoting high-value crops — kiwi, passion fruit, citrus
- **Jhum to Settled Cultivation:** Government program to transition jhum farmers to terrace farming + horticulture
- **NE Bamboo Mission:** Bamboo as crop and livelihood; processing industries

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Large Cardamom:** Nursery management; prepare seedlings for May transplanting
- **Ginger / Turmeric:** Harvest remaining crop; cure and store; prepare seed rhizomes for April planting
- **Orange (Khasi mandarin):** Post-harvest — grade, wax, and market; prune bearing trees after harvest
- **King Chilli / Bird's eye chilli:** Nursery preparation in protected structures; transplant in April
- **Potato:** Harvest in valley areas; store in cool conditions
- **General:** Apply lime 3–5 q/ha to neutralize soil acidity; construct bench terraces on slopes before monsoon; explore organic certification (NE is naturally organic — minimal chemical use)

## 25. Sikkim

### Geography & Climate
- **Agro-climatic zones:** Sub-tropical (300–1,500 m), Temperate (1,500–2,500 m), Alpine (>2,500 m)
- **Rainfall:** Annual 2,000–5,000 mm; varies by altitude
- **Temperature range:** 0–28°C depending on altitude
- **India's first 100% organic state** (since 2016)

### Major Crops
| Season | Months | Major Crops |
|---|---|---|
| Kharif | Jun–Oct | Rice, Maize, Large Cardamom (India's #1 producer), Ginger, Vegetables |
| Rabi | Nov–Mar | Wheat, Buckwheat, Mustard, Potato, Peas |
| Perennial | Year-round | Large Cardamom, Orange (Sikkim mandarin), Kiwi, Orchids, Tea (Temi tea) |

### Key Specialties
- **Large Cardamom:** India's top producer — 80%+ of national production; GI tag; grown at 1,000–2,000 m
- **Sikkim Organic Mission:** World's first 100% organic farming state; premium prices for all produce
- **Temi Tea:** Only tea garden in Sikkim; premium organic tea
- **Floriculture:** Cymbidium orchids — export to Europe

### Current Challenges
- **Climate change:** Large cardamom yields declining; disease (Chirkey & Foorkey viruses) increasing
- **Limited arable land:** Only 11% of area is cultivable; steep terrain
- **Marketing organic produce:** Premium price realization still a challenge despite organic certification

### Advisory for Current Season (Feb–Mar 2026)
- **Large Cardamom:** Pruning of shade trees; disease monitoring; prepare nurseries
- **Organic vegetables:** Protected cultivation nurseries for tomato, capsicum, peas
- **Orange:** Post-harvest care; pruning after harvest; organic manure application (5 kg vermicompost/tree)
- **General:** All inputs must be organic — use Jeevamrutha, Beejamrutha, Panchagavya per organic protocols
